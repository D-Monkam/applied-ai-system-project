Topic: Cat Enrichment and Well-being

Cats, especially indoor cats, need environmental enrichment to prevent boredom and stress. A stimulated cat is a happy cat.

- Interactive Play: Use wand toys, laser pointers (used responsibly), or feather toys to simulate hunting. Engage your cat in 10-15 minute play sessions a couple of times a day. This helps them express their natural predatory instincts.
- Vertical Spaces: Cats love to climb. Providing cat trees, shelves, or window perches gives them a sense of security and a vantage point to observe their surroundings.
- Scratching Posts: Scratching is a natural and necessary behavior for cats. It helps them stretch, mark their territory, and maintain their claws. Provide a variety of scratching surfaces, both vertical and horizontal.
- Puzzle Feeders: Instead of just leaving food in a bowl, use puzzle feeders. This makes mealtime more engaging and prevents overeating. It's a great way to provide mental stimulation.
- Grooming: While many cats are fastidious groomers, long-haired breeds like Persians require daily brushing to prevent mats and hairballs. Regular grooming is also a good bonding activity.
