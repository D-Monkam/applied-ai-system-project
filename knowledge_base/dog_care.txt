Topic: Comprehensive Dog Care Guide

**Enrichment and Exercise:**
High-energy breeds like Labradors, Golden Retrievers, and German Shepherds require at least 60-90 minutes of vigorous exercise daily. Lower-energy breeds like Basset Hounds or Bulldogs may only need 30 minutes of walking. A lack of stimulation can lead to boredom, anxiety, and destructive behaviors like chewing or digging.

- Daily Walks: All dogs need at least one 30-60 minute walk per day. This is crucial for both physical health and mental stimulation. Allowing the dog to sniff and explore their environment is a vital part of their walk.
- Play: Games like fetch, tug-of-war, or hide-and-seek are great for burning energy and strengthening your bond. A 15-20 minute session of vigorous play can be as beneficial as a short walk.
- Puzzle Toys: Food-dispensing puzzle toys (e.g., Kongs, snuffle mats) are excellent for mental enrichment. They challenge your dog to think and problem-solve, which can reduce anxiety and prevent boredom.
- Training: Regular training sessions (5-10 minutes daily) are a great way to engage your dog's brain. Teaching new tricks or reinforcing old ones provides mental exercise and improves their obedience.

**Diet and Nutrition:**
A balanced diet is the foundation of your dog's health. The right food depends on their age, breed, size, and activity level.

- Age-Appropriate Food: Puppies, adults, and senior dogs have different nutritional needs. Ensure you are feeding a formula designed for your dog's life stage.
- Portion Control: Obesity is a major health risk for dogs, leading to joint problems, diabetes, and heart disease. Follow the feeding guidelines on the food packaging and adjust based on your dog's body condition. You should be able to feel their ribs but not see them.
- Water: Always provide access to fresh, clean water. Dehydration can happen quickly, especially in warm weather or after exercise.

**Common Health and Wellness Issues:**
Regular vet check-ups are essential, but owners should also monitor for common problems.

- Fleas and Ticks: Use a veterinarian-recommended preventative treatment regularly. These parasites can cause skin irritation, allergic reactions, and transmit diseases.
- Dental Health: Dental disease is very common in dogs. Regular teeth brushing (with dog-specific toothpaste), dental chews, and professional cleanings can prevent painful gum disease and tooth loss.
- Ear Infections: Dogs with floppy ears are particularly prone to ear infections. Check their ears weekly for redness, odor, or discharge. Keep them clean and dry, especially after swimming or bathing.
- Separation Anxiety: Some dogs become anxious when left alone. Signs include barking, destructive behavior, or having accidents indoors. Crate training, providing puzzle toys, and gradual departures can help manage this.
